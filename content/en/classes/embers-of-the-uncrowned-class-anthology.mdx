<Section icon={Shield} title="Executioner: The Brutal Vanguard">

The Executioner stands as the cornerstone of the **embers of the uncrowned class anthology** for players who enjoy heavy melee combat. Armed with a massive axe, this class excels at frontline pressure and breaking through enemy defenses. The core loop revolves around building **Rage** through basic attacks and specific skills, which eventually triggers a **Fury** state. In this state, the Executioner gains access to explosive skill chains and increased movement speed, turning them into a relentless force on the battlefield.

| Build Path | Primary Element | Combat Style | Key Advantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sunderer** | Physical | Defense Shred | High boss damage via Shock |
| **Wild Hunter** | Physical | AoE Mobility | Fast clearing with critical hits |
| **Igniter** | Fire | Burn Stacking | Damage over time in close range |
| **Flame Pillar** | Fire | Ranged AoE | Safe fire pressure from mid-range |

The Sunderer path is particularly effective during the demo's Raid encounters. By applying the Shock status effect, the Executioner can reduce a boss's physical resistance, significantly boosting the party's overall damage output. Meanwhile, the fire-based builds offer better utility for clearing the elven invasion forces in the Amber Coast settlement.

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
While the Executioner is durable, its heavy attacks often have long recovery frames. Always keep a portion of your SP (Stamina) reserved for emergency evades, especially when fighting Domain Bosses like those found in the Goldenhaven region.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Spectral Blade: Shadow and Steel">

For those who prioritize speed and finesse, the Spectral Blade is the most mechanically intensive entry in the **embers of the uncrowned class anthology**. This class utilizes **Shade** as its primary resource, which is generated through swift strikes and consumed to unleash devastating finishing moves. The hallmark of the Spectral Blade is the ability to summon phantoms—spectral echoes that mimic the player's attacks or provide tactical distractions.

| Mechanic | Description | Player Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shade Generation** | Gained via successful hits/dodges | Fuels high-damage finishers |
| **Phantom Step** | Short-range teleport with Shade | Superior positioning and evasion |
| **Stealth** | Temporary invisibility | Guarantees critical Rear Strikes |
| **Darkness Echo** | Phantoms repeat the last skill | Doubles damage/utility output |

Mastering the Spectral Blade requires a deep understanding of "Rear Strikes." Positioning yourself behind an enemy provides a massive damage multiplier, which is essential for taking down high-health targets in the Towerstead alliances. The Darkness-based builds focus more on phantom control and area denial, while Physical builds emphasize raw assassination speed and single-target lethality.

<Callout type="info" title="High Skill Ceiling">
The Spectral Blade has a "High" difficulty rating. In the June 2026 demo, players who master the timing of Phantom Step can effectively "dance" through boss patterns, but a single mistake often leads to a quick death due to the class's lower defensive stats.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Stormbringer: Master of the Elements">

The Stormbringer rounds out the **embers of the uncrowned class anthology** by providing essential magical damage. By manipulating Wind and Lightning, this class controls the flow of battle from a distance. The Stormbringer is unique in its ability to create "safe zones" for the party using wind barriers or to paralyze entire groups of enemies with chain lightning.

| Skill Type | Elemental Focus | Effect | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cyclone** | Wind | Crowd Control | Grouping enemies for AoE |
| **Volt Bolt** | Lightning | Single Target | High burst against elite mobs |
| **Storm Wall** | Wind | Projectile Shield | Protecting the party from archers |
| **Thunder Call** | Lightning | Stun/Stagger | Interrupting dangerous boss casts |

In the demo's "Defense of Spring" encounters, the Stormbringer is indispensable. Their ability to group enemies with Wind skills allows the Executioner to maximize their AoE damage, while Lightning skills provide the necessary stagger damage to interrupt boss ultimates. The Wind builds generally offer more utility and positioning tools, whereas Lightning builds are the gold standard for magical burst damage.

<Callout type="success" title="Elemental Synergy">
Try to combine Wind skills to pull enemies into a central point before unleashing a high-damage Lightning spell. This "Wortex" combo is the most efficient way to clear large packs of enemies in the liberated regions of the map.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Character Progression: Steps to Power">

Building a character in the **embers of the uncrowned class anthology** involves more than just leveling up. The game features a layered progression system that integrates gear, skills, and ancestral power. Following the June 13, 2026 demo launch, it has become clear that "Infinite Progression" is centered on the synergy between these systems.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Define Your Combat Style">
    Select your class and choose a specific elemental or physical focus within your skill tree. Each class has nearly 30 skills, so focus on 5-6 core active skills that complement each other.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Socket Skill Gems">
    As you reclaim House Harborwell, you will find Skill Gems. These gems modify your active skills, adding effects like reduced cooldowns, increased AoE, or additional elemental damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enhance Gear and Weapons">
    Utilize the settlement's crafting facilities to upgrade your equipment. Higher-tier gear provides the base stats necessary to survive the 12 boss monsters featured in the demo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Awaken Your Bloodline">
    The Bloodline Awakening system connects your character to the legacy of the Uncrowned. This provides permanent passive stat boosts and unique abilities that trigger during combat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Recruit and Grow Companions">
    Bond with companions like Eslyn. Their skills and affinity-based quests provide a second layer of support, offering synergies that bolster your chosen class build.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Progression Priority">
In the early hours of the demo, focus on upgrading your weapon and unlocking your first four active skills. The "Settlement Growth" system will eventually unlock more advanced enhancement options as you upgrade buildings from a camp to a city.
</Callout>

</Section>
