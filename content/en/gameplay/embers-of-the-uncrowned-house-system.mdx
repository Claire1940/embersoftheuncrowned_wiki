<Section icon={Shield} title="Rebuilding the Core: Camp to Fortress City">

Reclaiming House Harborwell requires systematic upgrading of seven core buildings. Each facility governs a distinct aspect of character progression, from refining Skill Gems to unlocking group bonuses for raids. Leveling up the main Keep increases the building cap for all other structures, meaning your home base dictates the pacing of your entire operation.

Managing these buildings requires a steady influx of gold, timber, and iron ore. Striking a balance between building upgrades and direct gear enhancement is crucial, as overfunding your settlement can temporarily stall your character's combat power.

| Building Type | Core Function | Upgrade Priority | Benefit to Player |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Forge** | Gear enhancement and blacksmithing | **High** | Increases physical and elemental attack power |
| **Laboratory** | Skill Gem research and upgrades | **High** | Modifies active class skills and passives |
| **Trading Post** | Player economy and NPC trade | **Medium** | Generates passive gold and rare crafting components |
| **Sanctuary** | Companion resting and training | **Medium** | Increases companion affinity gains and stats |
| **Guild Hall** | Raid coordination and buffs | **Low** | Grants passive combat boosts for party encounters |
| **Outpost** | Resource gathering and dispatch | **Medium** | Sends idle companions to harvest region materials |
| **Keep** | Main house progression node | **High** | Unlocks higher tiers for all other settlement buildings |

<Callout type="tip" title="Keep Upgrade Strategy">
It is highly recommended to upgrade your Keep as soon as resources permit. The level of your Keep acts as a hard limit for all other facilities, preventing you from accessing advanced gear tiers.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Region Liberation & Resource Management">

The world is divided into 3 main regions, which contain a combined total of 10 sub-regions available for exploration and liberation. Reclaiming these territories from enemy forces is the only way to establish resource nodes and supply chains for House Harborwell. Securing these zones requires players to complete main story chapters, clear regional outposts, and defeat powerful Domain Bosses.

Once a region is cleared of threat, you can construct an Outpost to begin extracting specialized materials. These materials are shipped back to the settlement automatically, providing a steady stream of crafting ingredients.

| Region Name | Explorable Zones | Primary Resource | Primary Threat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Goldenhaven** | 3 Zones | Iron Ore, Timber | Corrupted vanguard troops |
| **Amber Coast** | 4 Zones | Sea Salt, Amber Shards | Domain Bosses, Elven invaders |
| **Towerstead** | 3 Zones | Arcane Crystals, Relics | Rifts, Defense of Spring events |

To secure resources and establish a reliable supply network, follow these core steps for territory acquisition:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear Sector Outposts">
    Defeat localized enemy camps scattered throughout the targeted region. This reduces the threat meter and reveals the location of the local Domain Boss.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Defeat the Domain Boss">
    Coordinate with other players or bring high-affinity companions to take down the Domain Boss. Defeating them clears the corruption from the region.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Establish the Settlement Node">
    Locate the ruined outpost in the liberated region and claim it for House Harborwell. This unlocks local resource generation and fast travel points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy Resource Gatherers">
    Assign idle companions or settlement workers to the node to harvest raw materials. These materials are shipped back to the main city automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Resource Yields">
Higher danger zones generate rarer resources such as Arcane Crystals, which are required for high-tier Skill Gem progression.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Companion Integration & Affinity Systems">

Companions are not merely passive followers or simple combat pets; they are deeply integrated into the management of House Harborwell. Recruiting allies like Eslyn opens up new ways to optimize your settlement facilities. Assigning the right companion to a building provides significant efficiency boosts, such as reduced crafting times or increased resource yields.

Building affinity with your companions by completing exclusive questlines and gifting them regional items unlocks their latent passive skills. These skills improve both their combat support capabilities and their effectiveness when managing structures in your city.

| Companion | Combat Role | Settlement Specialization | Synergy Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Eslyn** | Defensive Support | Laboratory Research | Reduces Skill Gem crafting time by 15% |
| **Aethelgard** | Vanguard Melee | Forge Operations | Decreases gold cost for gear enhancement |
| **Valeria** | Ranged DPS | Outpost Management | Increases resource collection speed by 10% |

The developmental lifecycle of your stronghold typically moves through three primary phases:
